MISA Coffee Estate Overview
---------------------------
The MISA coffee estate is divided into over 40 sub-areas, known locally as "Patte." Each Patte has its own extent in acres and the number of coffee plants planted in that area.

Each Patte is individually managed but follows a common estate-wide seasonal calendar for operations.


Monthly Coffee Estate Work Calendar (Jan–Dec)
---------------------------------------------

🗓️ January
- Activity: Weeding & Surface Cleaning
- Labor: 1 worker per 0.5 acre
- Machines: Brush cutter (if terrain permits)

🗓️ February
- Activity: First round of manure application (organic)
- Labor: 2 workers per acre
- Machines: None

🗓️ March
- Activity: Irrigation setup / Mulching
- Labor: 2 workers per acre
- Machines: Drip pipes (if available)

🗓️ April
- Activity: Blossom spraying (flowering stage)
- Labor: 1 sprayer per acre
- Machines: Motor sprayer or knapsack sprayer

🗓️ May
- Activity: Berry setting spray / Tipping
- Labor: 1 worker per acre
- Machines: Knapsack sprayer

🗓️ June
- Activity: Pre-monsoon nutrient spray / pruning
- Labor: 2 workers per acre
- Machines: Shears / pruners

🗓️ July
- Activity: Drainage maintenance / Shade regulation
- Labor: 2 workers per acre
- Machines: Spade, manual tools

🗓️ August
- Activity: Manual weeding / mulch topping
- Labor: 1–2 workers per acre
- Machines: None

🗓️ September
- Activity: Fertilizer application (post-monsoon)
- Labor: 2 workers per acre
- Machines: None

🗓️ October
- Activity: Harvest preparations, drying yard cleaning
- Labor: 1–2 workers per acre
- Machines: Drying yard tools

🗓️ November
- Activity: Cherry harvesting begins
- Labor: 4–5 workers per acre
- Machines: Harvest baskets, drying beds

🗓️ December
- Activity: Continue harvesting, initial drying, sorting
- Labor: 3–4 workers per acre
- Machines: Pulping unit (if available), drying racks


Notes:
- Labor is calculated per acre and should be scaled as per the area of each Patte.
- Rainfall, slope, and terrain may influence actual labor and machinery needs.
- Modern techniques and mechanization can reduce manual effort in select Patte areas.
